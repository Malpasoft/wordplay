Ir + A (Future) — Practice

1. Complete: "Voy _______ estudiar mañana."
Answer: a

Explanation: The structure is "ir + a + infinitive." The preposition "a" is required. "Voy a estudiar" = I'm going to study.

2. What form of "ir" goes with "nosotros"?
Answer: vamos

Explanation: "Nosotros" uses "vamos." Example: "Vamos a comer pizza" (We're going to eat pizza).

3. Complete: "¿Vas _______ viajar el próximo mes?"
Answer: a

Explanation: Like all ir + a constructions, you need "a" before the infinitive. This question asks "Are you going to travel next month?"

4. Which is correct for "ellos"?
Answer: Van a jugar

Explanation: For "ellos" (they), use "van." This means "They are going to play."

5. Complete: "No _______ a trabajar mañana."
Answer: voy

Explanation: To make ir + a negative, put "no" before the conjugated form of ir. "No voy a trabajar" = I'm not going to work.

6. Which time marker fits best: "Ella _______ este fin de semana."
Answer: va a descansar

Explanation: The pattern is always "conjugated ir + a + infinitive." This means "She is going to rest this weekend."

7. Complete: "Vamos _______ a comer pizza."
Answer: a (or "no" if negative)

Explanation: "Vamos a" is the nosotros form of ir + a. This sentence means "We're going to eat pizza." (Note: The sentence already has "a", so this tests understanding of the structure.)

8. What is the ir form for "yo"?
Answer: voy

Explanation: For "yo," the form is "voy." Example: "Voy a aprender inglés" (I'm going to learn English).
