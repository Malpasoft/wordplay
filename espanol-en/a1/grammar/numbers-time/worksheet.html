Numbers & Time — Practice

1. How do you say "17" in Spanish?
Answer: diecisiete

Explanation: Numbers 16-19 have special forms. Diecisiete = diez (10) + siete (7). Note it's written as one word, not "diez y siete."

2. How do you say "35" in Spanish?
Answer: treinta y cinco

Explanation: Numbers above 30 use "y" (and) between tens and ones. 35 = treinta (30) y (and) cinco (5).

3. What time is 3:00 in Spanish?
Answer: Son las tres

Explanation: For times after 1:00, use "son las" + number. 3:00 = "Son las tres" (They are the three o'clock).

4. What time is 1:00 in Spanish?
Answer: Es la una

Explanation: 1:00 is singular, so use "es la una" (it is the one o'clock). Only 1:00 is singular; all other times use "son las."

5. How do you say "91" in Spanish?
Answer: noventa y uno

Explanation: 91 = noventa (90) y (and) uno (1). Always use "y" between tens and ones.

6. What time is 4:30 in Spanish?
Answer: Son las cuatro y media

Explanation: For 30 minutes, use "y media" (and half), not "y treinta." 4:30 = "Son las cuatro y media."

7. What time is 5:15 in Spanish?
Answer: Son las cinco y cuarto

Explanation: For 15 minutes, use "y cuarto" (and quarter). 5:15 = "Son las cinco y cuarto."

8. How do you say "100" in Spanish?
Answer: cien

Explanation: 100 is "cien." When followed by another number (like 101), it becomes "ciento," but 100 alone is "cien."
