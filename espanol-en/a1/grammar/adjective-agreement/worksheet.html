Adjective Agreement — Practice

1. Complete: "La casa es _______ (blanco)."
Answer: blanca

Explanation: "Casa" is feminine singular, so the -o adjective changes to -a: blanca.

2. Choose the correct form: "los coches _______"
Answer: rojos

Explanation: "Coches" is masculine plural, so the adjective takes -os: los coches rojos.

3. Complete: "Mis hermanas son muy _______ (alto)."
Answer: altas

Explanation: "Hermanas" is feminine plural: alt + as = altas. The adjective agrees in both gender and number.

4. "Una pregunta _______" — which is correct?
Answer: fácil

Explanation: Adjectives ending in a consonant (fácil) do not change for gender. Singular noun → singular adjective: una pregunta fácil.

5. Make plural: "el examen difícil" → "los exámenes _______"
Answer: difíciles

Explanation: Consonant-final adjectives add -es in the plural: difícil → difíciles.

6. Juan y María son _______. (mixed group)
Answer: altos

Explanation: A mixed masculine + feminine group always takes the MASCULINE PLURAL: altos.

7. Where does the adjective usually go? "an interesting book" =
Answer: un libro interesante

Explanation: Spanish adjectives normally FOLLOW the noun: un libro interesante. Putting it before sounds literary or changes emphasis.

8. Complete: "Las películas son _______ (aburrido)."
Answer: aburridas

Explanation: "Películas" is feminine plural: aburrid + as = aburridas. After ser, the adjective agrees with the subject.
