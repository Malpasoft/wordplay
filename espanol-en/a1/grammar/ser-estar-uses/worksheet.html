Ser vs Estar in Depth — Practice

1. "Ella _____ ingeniera." (She is an engineer.)
Answer: es

Explanation: Use "ser" for professions and permanent identity. Her job as an engineer is a fixed characteristic.

2. "El gato _____ en el jardín." (The cat is in the garden.)
Answer: está

Explanation: Use "estar" for location. The cat can move to a different location.

3. "Yo _____ feliz hoy." (I am happy today.)
Answer: estoy

Explanation: Use "estar" for emotions and temporary states. Happiness is a temporary feeling, not a permanent identity.

4. "Los estudiantes _____ cansados." (The students are tired.)
Answer: están

Explanation: Use "estar" for temporary physical/emotional conditions. Being tired is a temporary state, not a permanent trait.

5. "Nosotros _____ de Argentina." (We are from Argentina.)
Answer: somos

Explanation: Use "ser" for origin and nationality. Where you're from is a permanent characteristic.

6. "La puerta _____ cerrada." (The door is closed.)
Answer: está

Explanation: Use "estar" with past participles (cerrada) to show the result of an action. Someone closed the door, and it is now in a closed state.

7. "Tú _____ muy inteligente." (You are very intelligent.)
Answer: eres

Explanation: Use "ser" for personality traits and permanent characteristics. Intelligence is a fixed quality of who you are.

8. "¿Dónde _____ ustedes ahora?" (Where are you all right now?)
Answer: están

Explanation: Use "estar" for location. Where someone is located is temporary and can change.
