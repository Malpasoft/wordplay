Conjunctions

because · although · so · however

Coordinating conjunctions

Join two equal clauses

and
addition: She works hard and she earns well.
but
contrast: He's smart but lazy.
or
alternative: Tea or coffee?
so
result: It rained, so we stayed home.
for
reason (formal): He was tired, for he had worked all day.

Subordinating conjunctions

One clause depends on the other

because
reason: I stayed home because I was ill.
although / even though
contrast/concession: Although it was cold, she wore a t-shirt.
when / while
time: I was reading when you called.
if
condition: If it rains, we'll stay home.
before / after / until
sequence: Wash your hands before eating.

Connectors (linking adverbs)

Connect ideas between sentences — more formal

however
contrast: It was expensive. However, it was worth it.
therefore
result: It rained. Therefore, we cancelled.
also / in addition
addition: She is kind. She is also very patient.
for example
example: She loves sport, for example swimming.
however and therefore connect SENTENCES (with full stop or semicolon). but and so connect CLAUSES within a sentence.

because vs because of

Common confusion — different grammar follows each

because + clause
I stayed home because I was ill.
+ subject + verb
She left because she was tired.
because of + noun
I stayed home because of my illness.
+ noun phrase
We were late because of traffic.

Common mistakes

Where learners go wrong most often

Although it rained, but we went.
→
Although it rained, we went.
No 'but' after although
Despite it rained, we went.
→
Despite the rain, we went.
Despite + noun phrase
He is tall so that he plays basketball.
→
He is tall so he plays basketball.
"So that" = purpose; "so" = result

although vs but / despite vs however

although (mid-sentence)
Although she was tired, she worked late.
but (joining clauses)
She was tired but she worked late.
despite / in spite of + noun/-ing
Despite being tired, she worked late.
however (new sentence)
She was tired. However, she worked late.

Recap

Addition
and · also · in addition
and she works hard
Contrast
but · although · however
but she's lazy / however, she works
Reason
because (+ clause) · because of (+ noun)
because I was ill
Result
so · therefore
so we stayed / therefore we cancelled
