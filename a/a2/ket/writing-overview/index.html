KET

Writing Overview

Part 7 · short message · 25–35 words

Format and timing

Part 7
Short message (email, note or postcard) · 25–35 words · 15 minutes
Content
You must cover all three content points given in the task
Total
1 task · 5 marks · ~8% of the paper

What the task looks like

You are given a short situation and three bullet points telling you what to write. You must address all three points in 25–35 words.

EXAMPLE TASK

You want to invite your friend Alex to visit you this weekend. Write a note to Alex.

Tell Alex:

  • when to come
  • how to get to your home
  • what you will do together

How to write a strong answer

  • Cover all three points. Missing one costs marks. Tick them off as you write.
  • Count your words. Under 25 loses marks. Over 35 wastes time and may include irrelevant content.
  • Use a friendly greeting and sign-off. "Hi [name]" and "See you soon!" are perfect for A2.
  • Keep it simple. Short, clear sentences score just as well as complex ones. Accuracy matters more than sophistication.
  • Check spelling. Spelling mistakes in short messages are easy to spot and lose marks.
Tip
The three content points are equally important. A beautifully written note that misses one point will lose marks. Always check all three before you finish.
