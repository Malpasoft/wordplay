To Be (present)

am / is / are · identity, place, feeling

What is "to be"?

Use to be to describe who you are, where you are, or how you feel.

Identity
I am a teacher. She is a student.
Place
We are at school. He is in London.
Feeling
I am happy. They are tired.
Age
She is 25 years old.
Note: In English, you can never drop the verb. You cannot say "I happy" — you must say "I am happy".

The three forms: am / is / are

I
I am Spanish.
you / we / they
You are kind. We are here. They are happy.
he / she / it
He is a doctor. She is tall. It is cold.

Contractions — natural English

I am → I'm
I'm a student.
you are → you're
You're right!
he is → he's
He's my brother.
she is → she's
She's my friend.
it is → it's
It's 7 o'clock.
we are → we're
We're ready.
they are → they're
They're here.

Negative — adding "not"

I am not
I am not tired. → I'm not tired.
you are not / aren't
You aren't late.
he/she/it is not / isn't
She isn't here.
we/they are not / aren't
They aren't ready.

Questions — switch the order

am / is / are + subject + ... ? ✓
Am I...?
Am I right?
Are you...?
Are you a teacher?
Is he/she/it...?
Is she your sister?
Are we/they...?
Are they at home?
Short answers
Yes, I am. / No, I'm not. (NOT Yes, I'm)

Common mistakes

I happy.
→
I am happy.
never drop "to be"
He are tired.
→
He is tired.
he/she/it → is
Are you Spanish? Yes, I'm.
→
Yes, I am.
positive short answer = no contraction
You is my friend.
→
You are my friend.
you → are (always)
