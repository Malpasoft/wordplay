Question Words

What · Who · Where · When · Why · How · Which

The seven question words

English uses seven main question words to ask for information. Each one asks for a specific type of answer.

What?
a thing or action — What is your job?
Who?
a person — Who is that?
Where?
a place — Where do you live?
When?
a time — When do you start work?
Why?
a reason — Why are you tired?
How?
a manner / method — How do you get to work?
Which?
choice between options — Which colour do you prefer?

Word order: question word + verb + subject

Auxiliary verb (do, does, is, are...) goes before the subject

Question word + auxiliary + subject + main verb?  →  Where do you live?
With "to be"
Where is she? · Who are they?
With do/does
What do you want? · Where does she work?
With can
How can I help you?

How + adjective/adverb

"How" combines with other words to ask specific questions

How old?
How old are you? — I'm 25.
How much?
How much is it? — €15.
How many?
How many students are there? — 24.
How often?
How often do you exercise? — Three times a week.
How long?
How long does it take? — About an hour.
How far?
How far is the station? — Two kilometres.
How much = uncountable (money, water). How many = countable (students, hours).

Who as subject vs object

Different word order when "who" is the subject

Who = SUBJECT
Who called?
no auxiliary needed
Who lives here?
verb has -s
Who = OBJECT
Who did you call?
auxiliary needed
Who do you live with?
do/does + base

Common mistakes

Where you live?
→
Where do you live?
Need auxiliary "do"
How many money?
→
How much money?
money = uncountable → much
What is mean "perro"?
→
What does "perro" mean?
Need does + base verb
When you started?
→
When did you start?
did + base verb (not past)
