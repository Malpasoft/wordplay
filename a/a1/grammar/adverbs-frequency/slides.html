Adverbs of Frequency

always · usually · sometimes · never

What are adverbs of frequency?

Adverbs of frequency tell us how often something happens. They go on a scale from always (100%) to never (0%).

always
100% — every time
usually
~90% — most of the time
often
~70% — many times
sometimes
~50% — occasionally
rarely / seldom
~10% — not often
never
0% — at no time

Position: before the main verb

With most verbs, the adverb goes BEFORE the verb

Subject + adverb + verb  →  I always drink coffee.
always
She always arrives on time.
usually
I usually walk to work.
often
They often go to the cinema.
never
He never eats meat.

Exception: after 'to be'

With am / is / are, the adverb goes AFTER the verb

Subject + be + adverb + ...  →  She is always late.
Before main verb
I always drink coffee.
She never eats meat.
After to be
I am always tired.
She is never late.

"sometimes" and "usually" are flexible

These two can also go at the beginning or end of a sentence

Beginning
Sometimes I work from home.
Middle
I sometimes work from home.
End
I work from home sometimes.
Always, never, often normally go in the middle. Don't put them at the end.

Questions with "How often?"

Use "how often" to ask about frequency

Question
How often do you exercise?
Frequency adverb
I usually exercise three times a week.
Time expression
I exercise three times a week / every day / twice a month.
How often do you read?
→ Every day.
How often does she travel?
→ Twice a year.

Common mistakes

I drink always coffee.
→
I always drink coffee.
Adverb before the main verb
She always is late.
→
She is always late.
After "to be" — exception
I go never to the cinema.
→
I never go to the cinema.
"Never" before the main verb
I don't never eat meat.
→
I never eat meat.
Never = already negative

Recap

Scale
always > usually > often > sometimes > rarely > never
100% to 0%
Main verb
adverb BEFORE verb
I always drink coffee.
to be
adverb AFTER verb
She is always late.
Question
How often + auxiliary + ...?
How often do you exercise?
