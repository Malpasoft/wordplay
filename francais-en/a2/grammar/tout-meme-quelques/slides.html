Quantifiers — tout, même, quelques, chaque

All, every, even, same, a few — common A2 quantifiers

Tout — All / Every

Tout is one of the most versatile French words. As an adjective, it agrees in gender and number.

Masc. sgFem. sgMasc. plFem. pl
touttouttoutetoustoutes
tout le monde — everyone (invariable)
toute la famille — the whole family
tous les jours — every day
toutes les semaines — every week

Même — Same / Even

Même has two uses: as an adjective (same) or as an adverb (even).

Adjective (same) — before noun:
le même livre — the same book
la même chose — the same thing

Adverb (even) — invariable:
Il travaille même le week-end. — He works even on weekends.
Elle ne mange même pas. — She doesn't even eat.

Quelques — A Few

Quelques is always plural and means a few / some.

J'ai quelques amis ici. — I have a few friends here.
Nous resterons quelques jours. — We'll stay for a few days.
Il reste quelques places. — There are a few seats left.
Always plural — never use with singular nouns.

Chaque — Each / Every

Chaque is always singular and means each / every. Always followed by a singular noun, no article needed.

chaque jour — each/every day
chaque élève — each pupil
chaque semaine — every week
Contrast: chaque jour (each day, no article) vs. tous les jours (every day, with article).

Quick Summary

Quantifier quick reference.

• tout agrees: tout/toute/tous/toutes.
• tout le monde = everyone (invariable, singular verb).
• même as adj. = same (before noun); as adverb = even (invariable).
• quelques = a few (always plural).
• chaque = each/every (always singular, no article).
