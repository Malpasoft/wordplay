In Context

Seeing B2 faux amis in authentic context cements the correct usage.

  • Il ignore que la réunion a été annulée. (He doesn't know the meeting was cancelled.) ✓
  • Elle prétend qu'elle était là. (She claims she was there.) ✓
  • Je ne peux pas supporter ce bruit. (I can't stand this noise.) ✓
  • Il m'a déçu. (He disappointed me.) ✓ NOT He deceived me.
  • Il est censé arriver à 10h. (He's supposed to arrive at 10.) ✓
  • C'est ma propre décision. (It's my own decision.) ✓

Common Mistakes

AvoidUseWhy
Je ne peux pas le supporter dans la réunion. (attend)Je ne peux pas le supporter = I can't stand him. (Je ne peux pas l'endurer)Supporter = tolerate/stand. To support someone in a meeting = soutenir quelqu'un.
Il prétend faire quelque chose = He pretends to do something?Il prétend faire quelque chose = He claims to do something.Prétendre = to claim. To pretend = faire semblant de.
Il ignore mes messages. (doesn't know / ignores?)Both are possible but different: ignorer = not know (usually) or deliberately ignore (context-dependent)In modern French, ignorer can mean both. Context clarifies: Il ignore la nouvelle (he doesn't know the news) vs Il ignore mes messages (he ignores my messages — deliberate).
Sensé vs censésensé = makes sense; censé = supposed toIl est sensé = it makes sense. Il est censé venir = he's supposed to come.
