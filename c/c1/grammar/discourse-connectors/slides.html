Discourse Connectors

Furthermore · nevertheless · conversely · subsequently

Discourse connectors at C1

At C1, you use sophisticated connectors to structure paragraphs and signal relationships across longer texts: addition, contrast, cause, result, sequence, summary.

Addition (formal)
furthermore · moreover · in addition · what is more
Contrast (formal)
however · nevertheless · nonetheless · on the contrary · conversely
Cause / result
consequently · therefore · thus · hence · as a result
Sequence
firstly · subsequently · ultimately · in turn
Summary
in conclusion · to sum up · on balance · all in all

Subtle contrast: however vs nevertheless vs nonetheless

however
most common, neutral contrast: The plan was bold. However, it failed.
nevertheless
"despite this": The plan was risky. Nevertheless, they proceeded.
nonetheless
same as nevertheless, slightly more formal: It rained. Nonetheless, the event went ahead.
on the contrary
"the opposite is true": He didn't fail. On the contrary, he excelled.
conversely
"in the opposite case": Big firms struggle in this market. Conversely, small firms thrive.

Cause and result: precise distinctions

therefore
"for this reason": Sales rose. Therefore, profits increased.
consequently
"as a direct consequence": Demand fell. Consequently, jobs were lost.
thus / hence
formal "therefore": The data is inconclusive. Thus, further research is needed.
accordingly
"following from this": New rules apply. Accordingly, all staff must comply.

Sequence and structure

first / firstly
Firstly, the data must be collected.
secondly / next
Secondly, it must be analysed.
subsequently
"after that": Subsequently, results are published.
in turn
"each one after the other": Each problem leads to another in turn.
ultimately / finally
Ultimately, the project succeeded.

Common mistakes

Conversely, the plan failed. (no opposite mentioned)
→
However, the plan failed.
"conversely" needs explicit opposite case
She is hardworking. On the contrary, she's lazy. (these contradict each other directly)
→
First statement should be the OPPOSITE: People think she's lazy. On the contrary, she's hardworking.
on the contrary = "opposite is true"
Therefore, but, the trend is positive.
→
However, the trend is positive.
don't combine contrasting connectors

Recap

Addition
furthermore, moreover, in addition
academic register
Contrast
however, nevertheless, conversely, on the contrary
choose by precise meaning
Cause/result
therefore, consequently, thus, hence
formal scholarly tone
Sequence
firstly, subsequently, ultimately, in turn
structuring arguments
