Gerunds & Infinitives (adv)

Meaning pairs · need + -ing · perfect forms

Beyond B2: subtle meaning changes

At C1 you handle the verbs where -ing vs to infinitive completely changes meaning — and the patterns where both forms exist with shifted connotation.

remember + -ing
I remember meeting her. (recall a past event)
remember + to
I remembered to call. (didn't forget)
come to + base
She came to realise her mistake. (gradual change)
come + -ing
She came running down the hall. (manner of arrival)

Subtle pairs at C1 level

go on + -ing
She went on talking. (continued the same activity)
go on + to
She went on to become CEO. (moved to new stage)
mean + -ing
Buying that car means working overtime. (involves)
mean + to
I meant to call. (intended)
regret + -ing
I regret saying that. (sorry about past action)
regret + to
We regret to inform you. (formal, present news)

Allow / permit / forbid — two patterns

+ object + to inf
They allow staff to leave early.
+ -ing (no object)
They allow leaving early. (the activity itself)
+ object + to inf
The law forbids people to smoke.
+ -ing (no object)
The law forbids smoking here.
Passive
Staff are allowed to leave. / Smoking is forbidden.

Need + -ing (passive sense)

need + -ing (passive)
My car needs cleaning. (= needs to be cleaned)
need + to inf (active)
I need to clean my car. (I do it)
want + -ing (older/British)
Your hair wants cutting. (= needs to be cut)
require + -ing
The report requires editing. (= needs to be edited)
need/want/require + -ing = passive meaning. Subject is the THING that receives the action. Common error: "my car needs to clean".

Perfect gerund and perfect infinitive

having + PP (perfect gerund)
Having finished his work, he went home.
to have + PP (perfect infinitive)
She seems to have left.
perfect passive infinitive
She was lucky to have been chosen.
perfect passive gerund
Having been told the news, she fainted.

Common mistakes

My car needs to clean.
→
My car needs cleaning.
need + -ing = passive meaning
She came to running down the hall.
→
She came running down the hall.
come + -ing (manner)
I regret to taking that decision.
→
I regret taking that decision.
past regret → regret + -ing
