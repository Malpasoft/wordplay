Attitudes & Opinions

controversial · sceptical · compelling · contentious · persuasive

Flashcards Lesson Game

Words 1–4

Controversial
causing disagreement and strong debate
Sceptical
not easily convinced; having doubts
Compelling
very interesting or persuasive; difficult to resist or deny
Contentious
likely to cause argument or disagreement
Examples
The policy is controversial because it affects many communities.
Experts remain sceptical about the long-term benefits.

Words 5–8

Persuasive
good at convincing people to believe or do something
Objective
not influenced by personal feelings; based on facts
Biased
showing an unfair preference for or against something
Impartial
treating all sides equally; not favouring one over another
Examples
The charity ran a persuasive campaign about climate action.
A good journalist should remain objective when reporting news.

Words 9–10

Cynical
believing that people are motivated only by self-interest
Credible
able to be believed; convincing and trustworthy
Examples
He was cynical about politicians' promises.
The government needs a credible plan to reduce emissions.
