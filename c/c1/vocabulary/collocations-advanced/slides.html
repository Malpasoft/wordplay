Collocations — Advanced

C1-level verb-noun and adjective-noun collocations

Flashcards Lesson Game

Words 1–4

Raise concerns
to express worries or doubts about something
Pose a threat
to present a danger or risk to something
Draw a conclusion
to arrive at a judgement based on evidence
Reach a consensus
for a group to arrive at a shared agreement
Examples
The report raised serious concerns about data security.
Antibiotic resistance poses a serious threat to public health.

Words 5–8

Exert pressure
to apply force or influence to make something happen
Spark a debate
to start or trigger a discussion or controversy
Bear a resemblance
to look similar to or share characteristics with something else
Come into effect
(of a law or policy) to start being applied
Examples
Lobby groups exerted pressure on the government to change the law.
The new policy sparked a heated debate in parliament.

Words 9–12

Make strides
to make significant progress towards a goal
Place emphasis
to give special importance to something
Harbour doubts
to hold private worries or uncertainties about something
Tackle an issue
to deal with a problem directly and energetically
Examples
The company has made great strides in reducing its emissions.
The curriculum places strong emphasis on critical thinking.
