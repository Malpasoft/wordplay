About the CPE

What is the CPE?

The Cambridge C2 Proficiency (formerly CPE) is the highest-level Cambridge English qualification. It certifies that candidates have a mastery of English at the level of an educated native speaker, suitable for postgraduate study and the most demanding professional contexts.

Exam structure

  • Reading and Use of English — 90 minutes · 7 parts · 53 questions (40% of total)
  • Writing — 90 minutes · 2 parts (compulsory essay 240–280 words + choice of 280–320 words) (20%)
  • Listening — 40 minutes · 4 parts · 30 questions (20%)
  • Speaking — 16 minutes · 3 parts · paired with another candidate (20%)

Scoring

The CPE is graded on the Cambridge English Scale from 162–230. A score of 200+ equals a passing grade C; 213–219 equals grade B; 220–230 equals grade A and corresponds to C2 mastery.
