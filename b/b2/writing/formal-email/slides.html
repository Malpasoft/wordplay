Formal Email / Letter at FCE

A formal email or letter is written to someone you do not know personally — a company, an official, an editor. Register, structure and appropriate phrases are all assessed.
Purpose
Complain, enquire, apply, request, suggest
Audience
Strangers — officials, employers, editors
Tone
Formal — no contractions, no colloquial language
Length
140–190 words

Structure

Salutation
Dear Sir / Madam · Dear Mr Smith · Dear Dr Jones
Opening line
State your purpose clearly in the first sentence
Main body
2–3 paragraphs — one idea per paragraph
Closing line
I look forward to hearing from you.
Sign-off
Yours faithfully (Sir/Madam) · Yours sincerely (named person)

Key formal phrases

Opening lines
I am writing with regard to...
I am writing to enquire about...
I am writing to draw your attention to...
Requesting / suggesting
I would be grateful if you could...
I would like to suggest that...
I would appreciate it if...

Complaining formally

I am writing to express my dissatisfaction with the service I received.
I was extremely disappointed to discover that...
I would be grateful if you could look into this matter as a matter of urgency.
I trust that you will take the necessary steps to resolve this issue.
Never be rude or aggressive — express dissatisfaction calmly and professionally.

Register — what to avoid

✗ Hi there! I wanna ask about the job.
✓ Dear Sir or Madam, I am writing to enquire about the vacancy advertised on your website.
✗ It's really bad and I'm so disappointed.
✓ I am writing to express my dissatisfaction with the quality of the product I received.
Contractions (don't, I'm, it's) are NOT used in formal writing.

Summary

Salutation
Dear Sir/Madam OR Dear + name
Opening
I am writing to / with regard to / to enquire about
Body
One clear idea per paragraph · no contractions
Closing
I look forward to hearing from you.
Sign-off
Yours faithfully (unknown) · Yours sincerely (named)
