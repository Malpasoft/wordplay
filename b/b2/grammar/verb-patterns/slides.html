Verb Patterns

Verb + to · verb + -ing · verb + obj + (to/bare)

Verb patterns at B2

B2 builds on A2/B1 by adding more complex patterns: verb + object + infinitive, verb + preposition + gerund, and report verbs with their own structures.

Verb + to-infinitive
decide, agree, promise, refuse, manage, fail, hope, expect, seem
Verb + gerund
enjoy, avoid, miss, deny, mind, suggest, finish, consider, imagine
Verb + object + to-infinitive
tell, ask, want, advise, encourage, persuade, force, warn, remind
Verb + object + bare infinitive
make, let, help (also possible with "to")

Verb + preposition + gerund

Critical B2 pattern — preposition forces -ing form

accuse of + -ing
She accused him of lying.
apologise for + -ing
He apologised for being late.
insist on + -ing
She insisted on paying the bill.
succeed in + -ing
He succeeded in passing the exam.
prevent (someone) from + -ing
The rain prevented us from going.
look forward to + -ing
I look forward to hearing from you.

Reporting verbs with their patterns

Each reporting verb has its own structure

admit + -ing
She admitted stealing the money.
deny + -ing
He denied taking the keys.
promise + to-inf
She promised to call.
refuse + to-inf
He refused to answer.
offer + to-inf
She offered to help.
threaten + to-inf
He threatened to leave.
suggest + -ing / that
She suggested going early. / She suggested that we leave early.

Causative patterns: make / let / have / get

make + obj + bare inf
They made him apologise. (forced)
let + obj + bare inf
She let me leave early. (allowed)
have + obj + bare inf
I'll have someone fix it. (arrange)
get + obj + to-inf
I'll get him to help us. (persuade)
have something done
I had my hair cut. (someone else did it)

Common mistakes

She accused him to lie.
→
She accused him of lying.
accuse + of + -ing
He admitted to steal the money.
→
He admitted stealing the money.
admit + -ing (not infinitive)
She made me to apologise.
→
She made me apologise.
make + object + bare infinitive
I look forward to hear from you.
→
I look forward to hearing from you.
"to" = preposition → -ing

Recap

Patterns
verb + to / verb + -ing / verb + obj + to / verb + obj + bare
memorise per verb
Prep + -ing
accuse of, apologise for, insist on, succeed in
always -ing after preposition
Reporting
admit/deny + -ing · promise/refuse + to-inf
she admitted stealing / promised to call
Causative
make/let + bare · get + to · have + obj + done
made him go / had my hair cut
