About the FCE Exam

Format · sections · scoring · what to expect

What is the FCE?

The Cambridge B2 First (FCE) is a high-intermediate English qualification. It proves you can communicate effectively in English at a B2 level — used for university admission, employment, and visa applications worldwide.

The four papers

Reading & Use of English
7 parts · 52 questions · 75 minutes · 40% of total mark
Writing
2 parts · ~140–190 words each · 80 minutes · 20% of total mark
Listening
4 parts · 30 questions · ~40 minutes · 20% of total mark
Speaking
4 parts · ~14 minutes · with a partner · 20% of total mark

Reading & Use of English — the 7 parts

Part 1
Multiple-choice cloze (vocab) · 8 questions
Part 2
Open cloze (grammar) · 8 questions
Part 3
Word formation · 8 questions
Part 4
Key word transformation · 6 questions
Part 5
Multiple choice (long text) · 6 questions
Part 6
Gapped text · 6 questions
Part 7
Multiple matching · 10 questions

Scoring and grades

  • Cambridge English Scale: 140–190
  • Grade A (180–190) → C1 level
  • Grade B (173–179) → strong B2
  • Grade C (160–172) → B2 pass
  • Below 160 → not passed (B1 level reported)
  • Each paper contributes 20% of the total — except Reading & Use of English at 40%

On the day

Tip
Reading & Use of English is the longest paper (75 minutes) and worth the most marks (40%). It rewards practice and time management. The Word Play FCE section covers all 7 parts in detail.
