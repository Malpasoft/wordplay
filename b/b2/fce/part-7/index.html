Part 7 — Multiple Matching

10 questions · match statements to short texts (A–E or A–F)

What does Part 7 test?

You see 10 statements/questions and 4–6 short texts. Match each statement to the text it relates to. Some texts will be used more than once. This tests scanning — finding specific information quickly.

Length
~700 words total across the texts
Questions
10
Time
~10 minutes (very fast — scanning skill)
Skills tested
Scanning, locating specific information, paraphrasing

Strategy

  • Read the 10 statements FIRST — underline key words
  • Skim each text quickly to get the topic
  • For each statement, scan the texts looking for the SPECIFIC information
  • Watch for paraphrases — a statement says praised for size and the text says remarkably big and won an award for its dimensions
  • Answer the easy ones first; come back to the hard ones
  • Some texts are used more than once — do not assume each text matches twice

Common pitfalls

Reading every word
You will run out of time. SCAN.
Trusting word repetition
A text that mentions expensive is not necessarily the answer for describes a high price — check for context.
Skipping the underlining
The key words in each statement guide your scan. Mark them.
