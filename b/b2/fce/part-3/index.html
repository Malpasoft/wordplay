Part 3 — Word Formation

8 questions · use the prompt word to form a derivative

What does Part 3 test?

Each line has a gap, and at the end of the line is a CAPITAL word. You must change that word to fit the gap — adding prefixes, suffixes, or changing the part of speech.

Length
~150 words
Questions
8
Time
~10 minutes
Skills tested
Suffixes, prefixes, conversion (noun ↔ verb ↔ adjective ↔ adverb)

Common transformations

Verb → Noun
decide → decision · arrive → arrival · achieve → achievement
Adjective → Noun
happy → happiness · safe → safety · weak → weakness
Noun → Adjective
beauty → beautiful · success → successful · economy → economic
Adjective → Adverb
quick → quickly · careful → carefully · good → well
Negative prefixes
happy → unhappy · possible → impossible · regular → irregular · responsible → irresponsible · agree → disagree
Other prefixes
understand → misunderstand · estimate → underestimate / overestimate

Strategy

  • Read the FULL sentence to know what part of speech is needed
  • Look BEFORE the gap (article? adverb?) and AFTER the gap (noun? preposition?)
  • Decide: noun? verb? adjective? adverb? Then form it
  • Check if it is positive or negative — many answers need a negative prefix
  • Check if it is plural (with -s) or singular
  • Spelling: double consonants (success → successful), drop -e (write → writer)
