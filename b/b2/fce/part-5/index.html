Part 5 — Multiple Choice

6 questions · long text · A/B/C/D options · reading comprehension

What does Part 5 test?

You read a long text (~700 words) and answer 6 multiple-choice questions about it. Questions test detailed comprehension, opinion, attitude, and reference (what does this/it/they refer to?).

Length
~700 words
Questions
6
Time
~12 minutes
Skills tested
Detail, gist, opinion, attitude, reference, vocabulary in context

Question types

Detail
What does the writer say about X?
Opinion/attitude
How does the writer feel about Y?
Reference
What does it / this / they refer to in line N?
Vocabulary
What does word X mean in this context?
Inference
What does the writer suggest about Z?
Gist/main idea
What is the main point of paragraph X?

Strategy

  • Skim the text first — get the gist (~2 minutes)
  • Read the questions BEFORE re-reading the text in detail
  • Questions follow the order of the text — Q1 is about the start
  • For each question, find the relevant section and read carefully
  • Eliminate wrong options — they often contain ONE detail that does not match
  • Watch for paraphrasing — the right answer is rarely word-for-word from the text
FCE Strategy: Read the question stem first, then search the text for the answer section. This saves time and keeps you focused on relevant parts.

Common traps

Tip
Wrong options often contain a phrase from the text but slightly distort the meaning. ALWAYS check that the answer matches what the text actually says, not just what looks familiar.
