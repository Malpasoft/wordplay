Part 6 — Gapped Text

6 questions · choose the missing sentence (A–G)

What does Part 6 test?

A text with 6 sentences removed. You see 7 options (A–G); choose which sentence fits each gap. There is one extra sentence you do not need. This tests text cohesion — how sentences link together.

Length
~500 words
Questions
6
Time
~10 minutes
Skills tested
Text structure, cohesion, reference, linking

What to look for

Linkers
However, Therefore, In addition, Nevertheless — these tell you the relationship between sentences
Reference words
This / that / these / they / such — they refer back to something specific
Pronouns
A he/she/it must refer to someone or something already mentioned
Topic continuation
A sentence about football should fit between sentences also about football
Time markers
Later, after that, by the next day — show sequence

Strategy

  • Read the WHOLE main text first, ignoring the gaps — get the overall flow
  • Read each gap sentence-before AND sentence-after carefully
  • Look at each option (A–G): what topic? what reference words? what tense?
  • Match by linkage: an option starting with However must contradict the previous sentence
  • An option containing this idea needs an idea mentioned just before
  • After choosing 6, the leftover option should make NO sense in any gap — that confirms your answers

Hardest tip

Tip
The extra (unused) option is designed to look plausible. If you are torn between two options for a gap, look at vocabulary repetition and reference more carefully.
