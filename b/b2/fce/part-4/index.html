Part 4 — Key Word Transformation

6 questions · rewrite the sentence using a given word · 2–5 words

What does Part 4 test?

You see a sentence, then a key word (in capitals), then a second sentence with a gap. Complete the gap so the second sentence has the SAME meaning as the first, using the key word. Do NOT change the key word. Use between 2 and 5 words including the key word.

Format
Sentence + key WORD + sentence with gap
Questions
6 (each worth up to 2 marks — half marks possible)
Time
~12 minutes (slower than other parts)
Skills tested
Grammar transformations, vocabulary, paraphrasing

Common transformation patterns

Reported speech
He said, I will help → He said he WOULD help
Passive
They built the house → The house WAS built
Comparative
No one is taller → Everyone is SHORTER THAN him
Modal of obligation
You have to leave → You MUST leave
Used to
I went swimming as a child → I USED TO go swimming
Wish/regret
I regret eating that → I WISH I had not eaten that

Strategy

  • Read both sentences carefully — what changed?
  • Identify the structure being tested (passive? conditional? comparative?)
  • Count your words — must be 2 to 5 INCLUDING the key word
  • Do NOT change the key word — use it exactly as given
  • You can earn HALF MARKS — even partial answers help
  • Contractions count as 2 words (do not = 2 words; doesn’t still counts as 2)
