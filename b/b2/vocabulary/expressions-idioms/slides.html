Expressions and Idioms

In the long run, take into account, fall short of.

Fixed phrases · FCE Use of English · Context
B2 Vocabulary

Why expressions matter

FCE Use of English Part 1 (multiple choice cloze) often tests fixed expressions and idioms. The correct answer is often the only one that forms the right collocation or phrase.
on the other hand
introduces a contrasting point
in the long run
over a long period of time
take into account
consider a factor when deciding
make ends meet
earn just enough money to live on
keep up with
stay at the same pace as
come to terms with
accept something difficult

More useful expressions

bear in mind
remember to consider
get to the point
say the most important thing without delay
a matter of time
something that will certainly happen eventually
on the verge of
very close to something happening
fall short of
fail to meet a target or expectation
go hand in hand
be closely connected or always occur together

Examples in context

Switching to renewable energy costs more now, but in the long run it will save money.
Many families are struggling to make ends meet because of rising inequality.
It is only a matter of time before AI replaces many white-collar jobs.
Poverty and poor health tend to go hand in hand.
