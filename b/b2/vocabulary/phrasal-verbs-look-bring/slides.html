Phrasal Verbs — Look and Bring

Look into, look forward to, bring about, bring in.

Meaning · Examples · Sentence practice
B2 Vocabulary

Phrasal verbs with LOOK

look into
investigate or research something
look up
search for information / improve
look after
take care of someone or something
look forward to
feel excited about something in the future
look out for
watch for / be careful about
look down on
regard as inferior

Phrasal verbs with BRING

bring up
raise a topic / raise a child
bring about
cause something to happen
bring out
publish / reveal a quality
bring in
introduce a law or rule / earn money
bring down
reduce / cause to fall or collapse
bring forward
move to an earlier time / propose

Examples in context

She looked into the company's data privacy practices before accepting the job.
The new policy brought about significant changes to the welfare system.
He brought up the issue of inequality during the board meeting.
The government plans to bring in new legislation to tackle discrimination.

Summary

LOOK
look into · look up · look after · look forward to · look out for · look down on
BRING
bring up · bring about · bring out · bring in · bring down · bring forward
