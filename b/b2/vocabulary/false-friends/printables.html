False Friends & Confusables

Lesson Handout

What are false friends?

False friends are words that look similar to a word in another language but have a different meaning in English. These are common sources of error.

Confusable pairs

These pairs look or sound similar in English and are confused by many learners.

Name: ___________________________    Date: _______________

False friend or not? Choose the correct word (8 points)

1. She is a very ______ person — she always makes wise decisions under pressure.
☐ sensible☐ sensitive
2. The study ______ that automation has a significant impact on employment levels.
☐ shows☐ demonstrates actually
3. I went to the ______ to borrow some books on environmental law.
☐ library☐ bookshop
4. He ______ felt embarrassed when he mispronounced the word in front of the class.
☐ actually☐ eventually
5. The project failed many times, but ______ they found a sustainable solution.
☐ eventually☐ actually

Affect or effect? Raise or rise? (8 points)

6. Deforestation ______ biodiversity on a massive scale.
☐ affects☐ effects
7. The ______ of automation on the workforce has been significant.
☐ effect☐ affect
8. Carbon emissions have ______ sharply over the last decade.
☐ risen☐ raised
9. The company ______ awareness of mental health issues in the workplace.
☐ raised☐ rose
10. She ______ to sign the new contract without reading it first.
☐ refused☐ denied
11. He ______ having received the report, but his email clearly showed he had.
☐ denied☐ refused
12. Could you please ______ me to send the report before 5pm?
☐ remind☐ remember
13. I always ______ to check my carbon footprint before booking a flight.
☐ remember☐ remind

Discover or invent? Economic or economical? (6 points)

14. Marie Curie ______ radium — it existed in nature before she found it.
☐ discovered☐ invented
15. Alexander Graham Bell ______ the telephone — it did not exist before him.
☐ invented☐ discovered
16. This is the ______ car on the market — it uses very little fuel.
☐ most economical☐ most economic
