Word Formation — Nouns

FCE Part 3 · -tion, -ment, -ness, -ity suffixes.

FCE Use of English Part 3 · Suffixes · Derivation
B2 Vocabulary

Why word formation matters

FCE Use of English Part 3 always tests word formation. You are given a base word and must change it to fit the gap. Most gaps require nouns, adjectives, verbs or adverbs.
-tion / -sion
innovate → innovation · discriminate → discrimination
-ment
achieve → achievement · employ → employment
-ness
aware → awareness · weak → weakness
-ity
diverse → diversity · equal → equality
-ance / -ence
resist → resistance · depend → dependence
-er / -or / -ist
employ → employer · innovate → innovator

Key noun formations

employ → employment
also: unemployment (un- prefix)
vary → variation / variety
two possible nouns from vary
automate → automation
the process of automating
equal → equality
also: inequality (in- prefix)
diverse → diversity
the quality of being diverse
sustain → sustainability
the ability to be sustained

Strategy for Part 3

Step 1: Read the sentence for meaning. Step 2: Decide what form is needed (noun). Step 3: Check if negative prefix is needed (un-, in-, dis-). Step 4: Check for irregular forms.
There is growing ______ of climate change. (AWARE) → awareness
The ______ in the workforce has declined. (DIVERSE) → diversity
______ has risen since automation began. (EMPLOY) → Unemployment

Summary of noun suffixes

-tion / -sion
most common — from Latin-root verbs
-ment
from verbs — achievement, development
-ness
from adjectives — awareness, weakness
-ity
from adjectives — diversity, equality
-ance / -ence
from verbs — resistance, dependence
