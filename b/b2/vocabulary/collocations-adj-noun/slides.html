B2 Vocabulary

Adjective + Noun collocations

These adjective + noun combinations cannot be translated literally. Using wrong adjectives is a very common error at B2 level.
strong evidence
convincing proof — NOT powerful or heavy evidence
heavy traffic
a lot of traffic on the roads — NOT big or strong traffic
high unemployment
a large number of unemployed people — NOT big unemployment
deep concern
serious worry — NOT big concern
broad range
a wide variety — NOT big or large range
close relationship
a near connection — NOT near relationship

More collocations

sharp increase
a sudden, steep rise — NOT quick increase
growing awareness
increasing consciousness of an issue
widespread inequality
inequality affecting many areas
key issue
the most important problem
major breakthrough
a very significant discovery or development
crucial role
an extremely important function

Examples in context

There is strong evidence that automation leads to widespread redundancy.
There is growing awareness of the deep concern about data privacy.
The report shows a sharp increase in carbon emissions last year.
Renewable energy plays a crucial role in addressing the climate crisis.

Summary

Strong
strong evidence · strong argument · strong impact
Sharp
sharp increase · sharp decline · sharp contrast
Widespread
widespread inequality · widespread damage · widespread concern
Deep
deep concern · deep impact · deeply rooted
