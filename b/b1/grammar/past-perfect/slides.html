Past Perfect

had + PP · before another past event

What is the past perfect?

The past perfect describes an action that happened before another past action. It's the "past of the past".

Before another event
When I arrived, the film had already started.
Reason for past situation
She was tired because she had worked all night.
Narrative sequence
He had never seen snow before he moved to Sweden.

Form: had + past participle

subject + had + past participle  →  She had finished. ✓
All subjects
I / you / he / she / we / they — all use had (same)
Contraction
I'd finished · She'd left
Negative
hadn't + past participle
Question
Had + subject + past participle?

The two-event timeline

Past perfect = earlier action · Past simple = later action

When I arrived (past simple), the meeting had already started (past perfect). ✓
Earlier (PP)
She had studied for weeks.
Later (PS)
She passed the exam easily.
Combined
She passed easily because she had studied for weeks.
already, just, never, before, by the time, when — all common signal words for past perfect.

Past perfect vs past simple

Past perfect (earlier)
When she arrived, he had left.
he left → she arrived
I was hungry because I hadn't eaten.
Past simple (same time)
She arrived and he left.
both at same moment
She arrived and then he left.

Common mistakes

When she arrived, the party ended.
→
When she arrived, the party had ended.
party ended BEFORE she arrived → past perfect
I had seen him yesterday.
→
I saw him yesterday.
no earlier past event → just past simple
She has eaten before we arrived.
→
She had eaten before we arrived.
two past events → had (not has)
Had she told to me earlier, I had helped.
→
If she had told me earlier, I would have helped.
past conditional structure

Recap
