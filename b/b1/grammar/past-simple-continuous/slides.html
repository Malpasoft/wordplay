Past Simple & Continuous

Narrative · when vs while · stative verbs

Past simple vs past continuous: B1 view

At B1 you use these two tenses together in longer narratives. Understanding their interplay is key to natural storytelling.

Past simple
completed actions, the events of a story
Past continuous
background scene, interrupted actions, parallel actions
Was walking (background) → saw (event)
While she was cooking, he arrived.

Narrative use: setting the scene

Continuous = backdrop · Simple = events

Scene
It was raining. People were walking quickly.
Event 1
Suddenly, a car stopped.
Event 2
A man got out and shouted.
Event 3
Everyone turned to look.
Scene = past continuous. Each plot event = past simple. Switch between them naturally in storytelling.

when vs while (B1 depth)

when + event (simple)
She was reading when the lights went out.
when = moment of interruption
When I arrived, they were arguing.
while + background (continuous)
While she was sleeping, he cooked dinner.
while = during the ongoing action
I fell while I was running.

Stative verbs: no continuous (review)

Thinking verbs
know · believe · understand · think (= believe) · remember
Feeling verbs
love · hate · like · want · need · prefer
Perception
see · hear · smell · taste · feel (= sense)
Possession/being
have (= own) · belong to · be · seem · appear
Some stative verbs have an active meaning that DOES allow continuous: I was thinking about you (= mental process). Context decides.

Common mistakes

I was watching TV when my friend was calling.
→
I was watching TV when my friend called.
short interrupting event → past simple
While she was waiting, the bus arrived and she was getting on.
→
...the bus arrived and she got on.
sequence of short events → past simple
I was knowing the answer all along.
→
I knew the answer all along.
stative verb → past simple

Recap

Scene/background
past continuous
It was raining. People were walking.
Plot events
past simple
Suddenly a car stopped.
when
+ short event (simple)
when the phone rang
while
+ ongoing action (continuous)
while I was reading
