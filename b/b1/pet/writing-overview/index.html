PET

Writing Overview

Writing 1 · short message · Writing 2 · longer text

Format and timing

Writing 1
Short communicative message (email or note) · 35–45 words · ~10 minutes
Writing 2
Longer writing task (article or story) · about 100 words · ~30 minutes
Total
2 tasks · 40 minutes · 25% of the paper

Writing 1 — Short message

Similar to KET Part 7 but 35–45 words. You must cover three content points. Register is usually informal (note or email to a friend).

  • Cover all three content points — each one is essential
  • Use a greeting and sign-off appropriate to the situation
  • Count your words: aim for 38–42 to be safe
  • Simple, accurate sentences are better than complex ones with errors

Writing 2 — Longer task

You choose one task from two options. Tasks at B1 are typically:

Article
An engaging piece for a magazine or website — personal voice, clear structure, interesting opening
Story
A short narrative using the given first or last sentence — past tenses, clear sequence, interesting ending
  • Plan before you write: a quick outline saves time and improves structure
  • Use linking words: however, although, as a result, in addition
  • Aim for exactly 100 words — 90–110 is fine
  • Check for common errors: verb tenses, subject-verb agreement, punctuation
Tip
Read both Writing 2 options carefully before choosing. Pick the topic you have the most ideas for, not the one that looks easiest at first glance.
