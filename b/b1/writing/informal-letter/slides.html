Informal Letter

Greeting, register, friendly tone, sign-off.

Register · Greeting · Closing · Layout
B1 · Writing

What is an informal letter?

At B1 level you may be asked to write a letter or email to a friend, family member or pen pal. The register is friendly and personal — not formal.
Purpose
Share news, invite, apologise, give advice, reply
Audience
A friend, relative or someone you know well
Tone
Friendly, warm, conversational — contractions are fine
Length
140–190 words

Structure

Greeting
Dear [name], / Hi [name],
Opening
Refer to the last letter / ask how they are
Main body
2–3 paragraphs covering the key points in the task
Closing
Hope to hear from you soon. / Write back soon!
Sign-off
Love, / Best wishes, / Take care,

Useful phrases

Opening
Thanks so much for your last letter — it was great to hear from you!
Sorry I haven't written for so long — it's been a really busy month!
Inviting / suggesting
Why don't you come and visit me this summer?
How about meeting up next weekend?
Closing
Can't wait to hear your news. Write back soon!
Hope to see you soon. Take care!

Common mistakes to avoid

✗ Dear Sir, I am writing to inform you that...
Too formal — use Dear [name] for informal letters.
✓ Hi Sara, Thanks for your message — it made my day!
✗ Yours faithfully, [name]
This sign-off is formal. Use Love / Best wishes.
✓ Lots of love, / Best wishes, / Take care,

Summary

Greeting
Dear / Hi + first name
Opening
Reference to last contact / apology for not writing
Body
Cover all the task points — friendly and conversational
Closing
Hope to hear from you / Write back soon
Sign-off
Love / Best wishes / Take care — NOT Yours faithfully
