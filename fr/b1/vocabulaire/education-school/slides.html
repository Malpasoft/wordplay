B1 Vocabulary

Core education vocabulary

curriculum
the subjects taught at school or university
semester
a period of study lasting about six months
deadline
the date by which something must be completed
graduation
the ceremony when students receive their diplomas
diploma
a certificate awarded for completing a course
tuition
payment for education or instruction
The curriculum includes English, maths, and science.

Assessment and learning

essay
a short piece of writing about a specific subject
exam
a test to assess knowledge or skills
textbook
a book used for teaching a particular subject
lecture
a talk given to teach people about a subject
grade
a mark or score given for academic work
I need to write a 2,000-word essay on climate change.
The final exam covers everything we learned this year.

Academic phrases

attend classes
be present at school or university lessons
submit work
deliver homework or assignments to a teacher
pass an exam
succeed in a test or examination
fail a test
not do well enough in a test
graduate from
finish a course or degree

Key differences from French

British vs American
British: "university," "secondary school" / American: "college," "high school"
Tuition vs fees
Tuition = educational cost; fees = other charges. French uses "frais de scolarité"
Grade system
A (excellent), B (good), C (satisfactory), D (pass), F (fail). Different from French système.
Semester/term
Semester = 6 months at university. Term = period in UK schools.
