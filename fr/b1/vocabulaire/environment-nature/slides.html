Environment and Nature

Natural world, weather, ecosystems, and environmental issues.

Natural vocabulary · Environmental terms · Weather & landscapes
B1 Vocabulary

Natural landscapes and geography

These words describe natural features of the Earth:

  • Forest: a large area of land covered with many trees
  • Desert: a very dry area with little vegetation
  • Mountain: a very high landform with a peak
  • Valley: a low area between mountains or hills
  • Waterfall: water that falls down from a high place
  • River: a large stream of water flowing through land

Weather and climate vocabulary

Common words for weather phenomena:

Frostthin ice coating on surfaces
Hailfrozen rain that falls as small balls
Droughta long period without rain
Floodwater overflowing onto land
Breezea light, gentle wind
Hurricanea severe tropical storm

Environmental issues and conservation

Key environmental terms:

  • Pollution: contamination of the environment with harmful substances
  • Greenhouse gas: gas that traps heat in the atmosphere
  • Renewable energy: energy from sources that do not run out (solar, wind)
  • Biodiversity: the variety of living things in an area
  • Habitat: the natural home of a plant or animal
  • Species: a group of living things that can breed together

Animals and ecosystems

Types of animals:

Mammalan animal that feeds milk to its young
Reptilea cold-blooded creature with scales (snakes, lizards)
Birdan animal with wings and feathers
Fisha creature that lives in water with gills
Insecta small creature with six legs
Predatoran animal that hunts other animals for food

Plants and vegetation

Plant-related vocabulary:

  • Seedling: a young plant grown from a seed
  • Root: the part of a plant that absorbs water underground
  • Stem: the main structure supporting leaves and flowers
  • Leaf/Leaves: the flat structures that collect sunlight
  • Bloom: to flower or produce flowers
  • Weed: an unwanted plant growing in a garden

Key takeaway

Understanding environmental and nature vocabulary is essential for discussing the natural world and environmental issues. These words appear frequently in news, travel, and scientific contexts.
