Collocations: MAKE & DO

Common verb collocations in English.

Common mots · Phrase patterns · Verb + noun combinations
B1 Vocabulary

MAKE vs. DO: The difference

MAKE = produce, create, or cause something to exist

DO = perform an action or task

Both are very common but have different collocations — you cannot use them interchangeably.

Common collocations with MAKE

  • Make a decision — choose something after thinking
  • Make a mistake — do something incorrectly
  • Make progress — move forward or improve
  • Make a difference — have a positive effect
  • Make money — earn income
  • Make a plan — organise something
  • Make an effort — try hard
  • Make friends — form new friendships

Common collocations with DO

  • Do homework — complete schoolwork
  • Do exercise / sport — be physically active
  • Do the housework — clean the home
  • Do your best — try as hard as possible
  • Do a job — work in a position
  • Do research — investigate and study
  • Do a favour — help someone
  • Do well — succeed or perform at a high level

Comparing MAKE and DO

MAKE a decision (NOT do a decision)

We make the choice ourselves.

DO homework (NOT make homework)

We do the work that was assigned.

MAKE progress (NOT do progress)

Progress is the result of our actions.

DO your best (NOT make your best)

You perform at your highest level.

Exercices

Choose MAKE or DO:

1. I need to _____ my homework. (do)

2. She _____ a new friend at school. (made)

3. He _____ a lot of progress recently. (made)

4. I always _____ exercise in the morning. (do)

5. She _____ an effort to be on time. (made)

Key takeaway

MAKE and DO have different meanings and different collocations. Learning these together will help you speak more naturally.

When you see a collocation, try to remember: Is it made or done?
