Phrasal Verbs: GET & TAKE

Essential phrasal verbs using GET and TAKE in everyday English.

Phrasal verbs · Verb particles · Common combinations
B1 Vocabulary

What are phrasal verbs?

A phrasal verb is a verb + preposition/particle that creates a new meaning.

Example: get = obtain, but get up = wake and rise

Phrasal verbs cannot always be translated word-for-word. You must learn them as complete units.

Phrasal verbs with GET

Get upto wake and get out of bed"I get up at 7 am."
Get onto board a vehicle or have a good relationship"We get on very well."
Get awayto escape or take a holiday"She needs to get away."
Get backto return home"I got back late."
Get offto leave a vehicle or stop doing something"Get off at the next stop."
Get downto make sad; to move lower"Don't let it get you down."

Phrasal verbs with TAKE

Take offto remove clothing; to leave the ground (plane)"The plane takes off at 10 am."
Take onto accept responsibility or hire someone"She took on a new project."
Take overto gain control"He took over the company."
Take partto participate"Did you take part in the event?"
Take backto return something; to withdraw a statement"I take back what I said."
Take outto remove; to go on a date with"He took her out to dinner."

Separable vs. non-separable

Separable: Object can go between verb and particle.

  • "I took off my jacket" OR "I took my jacket off" ✓
  • Example: get up, take off, get down

Non-separable: Object must follow the particle.

  • "I got on the bus" (NOT "got the bus on") ✓
  • Example: get on, take part, get away

Practice sentences

Complete with the correct phrasal verb:

1. I always _____ _____ at 6 am. (get up)

2. The plane _____ _____ on time. (take off)

3. We _____ _____ very well. (get on)

4. She _____ _____ the project last month. (take on)

5. Would you like to _____ _____ ? (take part)

Key takeaway

Phrasal verbs are frequent in English conversation and writing. Learn them as complete units with examples, not just the translation.
