Present Perfect

Actions that happened in the past but are relevant now.

Have/has + past participle · Recent actions · Life experience
B1 Grammar

What is present perfect?

The present perfect connects the past to the present. It describes actions that happened in the past but have a result now.

Form: have/has + past participle

  • I have worked here for two years. (I still work here)
  • She has finished her homework. (The homework is done)
  • They have never visited France. (No experience visiting France)

Formation: present perfect

SubjectHave/HasPast Participle
I, you, we, theyhaveworked, seen, eaten
he, she, ithasworked, seen, eaten

Affirmative: I have worked

Negative: I have not worked (I haven't worked)

Question: Have you worked?

Uses of present perfect (1)

1. Recent actions (just, yet, already)

  • I have just finished dinner.
  • Have you finished yet? (question)
  • She has already arrived.

2. Actions with undefined time (never, ever)

  • I have never been to Japan.
  • He has ever tried sushi? (question)

Uses of present perfect (2)

3. Duration (for, since)

  • I have lived here for 5 years.
  • She has worked here since 2020.

4. Life experience (how many times)

  • I have visited three countries.
  • She has been to London twice.

Key takeaway

Present perfect connects past and present.

  • Use it for recent actions
  • Use it for life experience
  • Use it with "for" (duration)
  • Use it with "since" (start time)
  • Use it with "just", "already", "yet"

Do NOT use: Use simple past with specific times (yesterday, last week, in 1999)
