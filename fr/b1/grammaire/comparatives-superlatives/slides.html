Comparatives and Superlatives

Comparing two things and saying which is best.

Adjective forms · -er / -est endings · Irregular forms
B1 Grammar

Comparatives: comparing two things

Use comparatives to compare two people, things, or groups.

Structure: Subject + verb + comparative + than

  • Paris is bigger than Lyon.
  • She is more intelligent than he is.
  • This phone is more expensive than the other one.

Formation: one-syllable adjectives (add -er)

AdjectiveComparativeExample
talltallerHe is taller than his brother.
fastfasterCars are faster than bicycles.
coldcolderWinter is colder than autumn.
bigbiggerThis house is bigger than mine.

Note: Double the final consonant if the word ends in consonant + vowel + consonant.

Formation: two+ syllables (use "more")

AdjectiveComparativeExample
intelligentmore intelligentShe is more intelligent.
expensivemore expensiveGold is more expensive than silver.
comfortablemore comfortableThis chair is more comfortable.
beautifulmore beautifulNature is more beautiful there.

Superlatives: saying which is best/worst

Use superlatives when one thing is more than all others in a group.

Structure: Subject + verb + the + superlative

  • Paris is the biggest city in France.
  • She is the most intelligent student in the class.
  • This is the most expensive phone on the market.

Formation: superlatives

One syllableSuperlativeExample
tallthe tallestHe is the tallest person here.
bigthe biggestThis is the biggest house in town.
Two+ syllablesSuperlativeExample
intelligentthe most intelligentShe is the most intelligent.
expensivethe most expensiveThat is the most expensive item.

Irregular forms

PositiveComparativeSuperlative
goodbetterthe best
badworsethe worst
farfarther / furtherthe farthest / furthest
much / manymorethe most
littlelessthe least

Key takeaway

Remember the rules:

  • One syllable? Add -er/-est
  • Two+ syllables? Use more/most
  • Always use "than" with comparatives
  • Always use "the" with superlatives
  • Learn irregular forms!
